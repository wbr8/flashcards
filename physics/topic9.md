Q: When is the temperature of a material at absolute zero?
A: When the kinetic energy of all of its molecules is zero.

Q: When the kinetic energy is completely removed from all molecules of a substance, what is the temperature of the substance?
A: Absolute zero.

Q: What is absolute zero in kelvin?
A: $0 K$

Q: What is absolute zero in degrees celsius?
A: $-273.15 ^\circ$C

Q: What does the shape of the Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution show us?
A: 
- There are no molecules with zero energy
- Only a few molecules have high energy
- There is no maximum value for the energy a molecule can have

Q: A Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution graph is for...
A: ...one specific temperature. As the temperature changes, so the graph changes.

C: The peak on the Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution graph moves towards [higher] energies as the temperature increases.

Q: What is rms speed short for?
A: Root-mean-squared speed.

Q: What is the symbol for root-mean-square speed?
A: 
$$
\sqrt{\langle c^2 \rangle}
$$

Q: What has the symbol $\sqrt{\langle c^2 \rangle}$ ?
A: Root-mean-square speed

Q: How do you find the root-mean-square speed?
A: Square the individual speeds of a set of molecules, find the mean of the squares, then take the square root of that mean.

Q: What is the formula relating the average kinetic energy of molecules in a substance to the temperature of the substance?
A: 
$$
\frac{1}{2} m \langle c^2 \rangle = \frac{3}{2} k T
$$

Q: What is the value of the Boltzmann constant?
A: $1.38 \times 10^{-23} J K^{-1}$
